    Week One: Introduction (Aug 20-Aug 22)¶

    Tuesday: Welcome

    Reading:

    • This whole website.

    Thursday: Horror Media, Horror Method

    Our first task this semester is to develop a shared, critical vocabulary to use across the rest of the semester. We'll jump into it this week by asking the question: What do we mean when we say the term horror? Resist the urge to make Jones and Cavarero agree, and instead try to read each piece on its own terms. For all of our readings this semester, start by identifying each text's categories, relations between categories, method, and implications. Why is the author using these categories, in this way, with this method?

    Reading:

    • Darryl Jones, "Introduction," Horror: a very short introduction (Oxford University Press, 2021): 1-22.
    • Adriana Cavarero, Horrorism (Columbia University Press, 2011): 1-9.

    Week Two: Monster Theory (Aug 27-Aug 29)¶

    This week, we will extend our reading method to the question of monsters. It is easy to imagine that monster is a transhistorical category-- the category of the scary thing. But, as with horror, the concept of monster is historically specific. What is a monster? Is it a concrete thing, an abstract idea? What do monsters do? What is their utility, and how can we think historically ABOUT monsters, but also WITH monsters?

    Week Three: On Dreams (Sep 3-Sep 5)¶

    This week and next, we will develop our common analytical vocabulary further through Freud and psychoanalysis, as an introduction to the symptomatic reading of monsters, ghosts, and horror.

    Start with a basic knowledge of Id, Ego, Super-ego, and "return of the repressed". Another encyclopedia-like resource for psychoanalytic concepts: No Subejct.

    Week Five: Horror's Fantasies (Sep 17-Sep 19)¶

    Lots of viewing time this week so plan ahead. Watch Pervert’s Guide first so you can later start trying to figure out some of the errors (or, after Freud, we might say, “naive readings”) of the interviewees in Room 237. If you are familiar with The Shining, watch Pervert’s Guide then Room 237. If you are not familiar with The Shining watch: 1 Pervert’s Guide, 2 The Shining, 3 Room 237.

    Important: Be careful with the word “fantasy/phantasy” here. It does not mean something opposed to “reality.” In fact, in this case “reality” is produced by, and supported by, “phantasy”–you can find that on page 30-32 of Civilization & Its Discontents.
